Fast Facts

Scientific Name: Taeniopygia guttata
Average Lifespan: 4-6 years
Size: Small
Origin: Australia, Indonesia
Habitat: Savannas, subtropical dry habitats
Diet: Mainly seeds
Predators: Small mammals, snakes, birds of prey

Physical Appearance

Zebra Finches are diurnal, meaning that males and females can be told apart based on their appearance.

Males

  • usually gray colored with a white cream/underside
  • orange beaks
  • black and white barred tail
  • red eyes

Females

  • usually gray colored with a white cream/underside
  • red-orange beaks
  • black and white barred tail
  • red eyes
  • striped throat
  • red-orange cheek patches
  • brown feathers with white spots on sides

Behavior

Zebra finches are very social, living in large flocks. During breeding, however, large groups break into smaller ones that remain in contact. Members of their group are recognized by their songs, allowing friendly members to visit their nesting sites and chasing away members of foreign groups. They are very territorial but the defended area (just outside the nest) is usually very small.

Diet

Zebra finches mostly eat seeds, but they also enjoy various fruits and vegetables and occasionally small insects.

Predators

  • Small marsupials (i.e. mice)
  • Diurnal (as opposed to nocturnal) birds of prey
  • Snakes
